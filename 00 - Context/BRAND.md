## Voice

**Personality:** Capable. Clear. Grounded.

**We sound like:** A sharp operator who has tested everything and tells you what actually works. Confident, not arrogant. Practical, not preachy.

**We don't sound like:** A futurist giving a TED talk. A hype machine selling the AI dream. A generic content mill repackaging the same "Top 10 AI Tools" list. Salesy or desperate.

### Principles

1. **Lead with outcomes, not technology.** We sell time saved, processes fixed, and results achieved. AI is the how, not the what.
2. **Be direct.** No fluff. Say what the tool does, what it costs the reader in time, and what they'll get.
3. **Make AI feel normal.** Practical tool, not a threat. No jargon.
4. **Show, don't tell.** Every playbook and article is based on things we actually built and tested. That's our credibility.
5. **Be opinionated.** We test tools and tell readers what works and what doesn't. We don't hedge with "it depends on your use case" when we have a clear answer.

### Do / Don't Examples

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "I tested 4 follow-up automation tools. Only one handled multi-step sequences without breaking." | "There are many AI tools available for automating follow-ups. Each has its own strengths and weaknesses." |
| "This playbook walks you through setting up automated client updates in under 2 hours." | "We leverage cutting-edge agentic AI frameworks to optimize operational throughput." |
| "Here's the exact prompt, the exact settings, and what to do when it doesn't work the first time." | "Our autonomous agents leverage large language models to execute multi-step reasoning chains." |

---

## Copy Rules

- **No em dashes** in any public-facing content (website, marketing, playbooks, articles, outreach).
- **No jargon.** Avoid: agentic frameworks, LLMs, multi-step reasoning, RAG, embeddings, autonomous systems. Use plain language.
- **Outcomes first.** Every piece of copy should answer "what does the reader get?" before explaining how.
- **Be specific.** Use numbers, timeframes, and concrete results instead of vague promises.
