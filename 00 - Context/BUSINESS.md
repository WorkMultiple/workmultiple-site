# WorkMultiple - Business Overview

> **What WorkMultiple is, who it serves, and how it makes money.** This file gives any agent or collaborator the business context needed to represent WorkMultiple accurately.

---

## What We Are

**Type:** Content-driven authority brand
**One-liner:** WorkMultiple helps non-technical people use AI to get more done, through premium playbooks, practical free content, and a growing library of tested workflows.

The tagline "More done. Same team." still applies. The shift is in how we deliver on that promise: instead of building automation systems for one client at a time, we document the builds as step-by-step playbooks anyone can follow. We publish the research and testing behind those builds as free content that drives organic traffic and affiliate revenue.

---

## The Pivot (Context)

WorkMultiple started as a done-for-you AI automation agency targeting high-ticket contractors. Over the course of building the agency (brand, website, ICP, pricing, sales process, plugin architecture), the founder realized the agency model was misaligned with his strengths and preferences:

1. Each client engagement was fully custom (opposite of "build once, sell many").
2. The sales process required heavy outbound and two-call closes. The founder is a marketer, not a salesperson.
3. Key contractor tools (Buildertrend, ServiceTitan) lack MCP connectors, creating integration gaps outside of the founder's control.
4. The stress of managing bespoke client engagements was unsustainable before even landing the first client.

All the work from the agency era (ICP, pricing, sales process, plugin architecture, brand) now serves as source material for the first premium playbook. Nothing was wasted.

## Target Audience

**Primary:** Non-technical founders, entrepreneurs, small business operators, and employees who want to use AI beyond ChatGPT but don't know how.

**What they have in common:**

- They want to speed up processes or growth using AI.
- They don't want to be left behind as AI transforms how businesses operate.
- They don't know where to start beyond basic ChatGPT usage.
- They are willing to pay for implementation-ready guidance that saves them weeks of trial and error.
- They are not developers. They cannot code their own solutions.

**What they care about:** Practical results. Time saved. Processes that actually work. Clear instructions they can follow without a technical background. They do NOT care about the underlying technology. They care about what it does for them.

## The Business Model: The Ecosystem Approach

### Phase 1: The Playbook Engine (Short-Term Revenue)

Premium step-by-step playbooks priced at $197 to $497 each. Each playbook solves one specific business problem using AI tools. They are implementation-ready: exact steps, exact tool settings, exact prompts, screenshots of every screen, troubleshooting guides. The buyer is paying for 20+ hours of trial-and-error they don't have to do.

**Revenue target:** 10 customers at $2K each, or 40 customers at $500 each = $20K. Achievable once a library of 8 to 10 playbooks exists with steady organic traffic.

### Phase 2: The Authority Hub (Long-Term Growth)

Free content published on the WorkMultiple site: deep-dive comparisons, tool reviews, practical tutorials. This is NOT generic "Top 10 AI Tools" content. It is opinionated, tested, practical content like "I tried to automate client follow-up with 4 different AI tools. Here's what actually worked."

Free content drives SEO traffic. Each article includes affiliate links for tools reviewed. Each article also serves as top-of-funnel that points readers to relevant premium playbooks.

### Phase 3: Membership Flip (Scale)

Once there are 5 to 10 solid playbooks, flip the model into a $49/month premium community. Gate the playbooks and ongoing tool reviews behind a recurring subscription.

**Target:** 100 members at $49/month = $4,900/month recurring, plus affiliate commissions that compound as traffic grows.

## Competitive Positioning

**What the market looks like:**

| Typical AI content | WorkMultiple |
|---|---|
| "Top 10 AI Tools for Business" listicles | "I tested 4 AI follow-up tools. Here's what actually worked for a 10-person team." |
| Generic ChatGPT prompt collections | Implementation-ready playbooks with exact steps, settings, and screenshots |
| YouTube tutorials that skip the hard parts | Written guides that include troubleshooting, edge cases, and what to do when things break |
| Platform-locked content (only works with one tool) | Tool-agnostic reviews that test across platforms and recommend what actually performs |

**The differentiator:** Tool and platform agnosticism. WorkMultiple tests everything and tells you what actually works for specific business problems. The market is flooded with surface-level AI content. The void is practical, technical, step-by-step implementation that tests tools against real business outcomes.
