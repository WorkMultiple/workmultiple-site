# WorkMultiple - Decisions Log

> **These decisions are settled.** Do not revisit them unless Emilio explicitly asks to reopen one. When a new decision is made during a session, add it to the bottom of this table.

---

| Date | Decision | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| 2026-03-04 | Pivot from agency to playbook/authority model | Agency model misaligned with founder's strengths and preferences. Each engagement was fully custom (opposite of build-once-sell-many). Sales process required heavy outbound and two-call closes. Contractor tools lacked MCP connectors. Client management stress was unsustainable. Playbook model is marketing-driven, build-once-sell-many, no client management. |
| 2026-03-04 | Agency work becomes first playbook content | All materials from the agency build (ICP, pricing, sales process, plugin architecture, brand) serve as source content for the first premium playbook: "How to Build a Done-for-You AI Automation Agency." Nothing was wasted. |
| 2026-03-04 | Brand, domain, and visual identity carry forward | WorkMultiple name, workmultiple.com domain, "More done. Same team." tagline, brand voice (Capable. Clear. Grounded.), visual identity (light theme, Inter, coral #e9594a, Stripe/Linear aesthetic), and copy rules (no em dashes, no jargon, outcomes first) all carry forward unchanged. |
| 2026-03-04 | No outbound sales; inbound only via content and SEO | The founder is a marketer, not a salesperson. No cold emails, no DMs, no sales calls. All traffic and sales come through published content, SEO, community engagement, and word of mouth. |
| 2026-03-04 | Playbook pricing: $197 to $497 | Standard playbooks at $197. Deep-dive playbooks at $297 to $497. Pricing reflects time saved (20+ hours of trial and error), not document length. |
| 2026-03-04 | First playbook priced at $297 to $497 | Given the depth of material from the agency build. Covers niche selection, ICP development, pricing strategy, sales process design, technical architecture, client delivery, and the integration landscape. |
| 2026-03-04 | Early distribution: Reddit, Indie Hackers, Twitter/X, LinkedIn | No paid ads. No YouTube. No podcast. Post useful content in communities, build credibility, link back to the site. Marketing, not sales. No DMs or outbound. |
| 2026-03-04 | Payment platform TBD | Evaluating Gumroad, LemonSqueezy, and Stripe. Decision deferred to Phase 3 when the first playbook is ready to sell. Ease of setup wins. |
| 2026-03-04 | Email platform: Beehiiv | Beehiiv selected as the newsletter and email platform. Must be live at site launch with an automated welcome sequence. No spreadsheet intermediary. Chosen for simplicity and cost at low volume. |
| 2026-03-04 | X account created: @WorkMultipleAI | x.com/WorkMultipleAI. Used for build-in-public updates, article distribution, thread breakdowns of tools tested, and community engagement. |
| 2026-03-04 | Lead with free content before selling the first playbook | Playbook assembly runs in parallel with content creation, but the playbook does not go on sale until the site has at least 2 to 3 published articles and an initial email audience. Free value and community presence come first. |
| 2026-03-04 | Publish 3 high-quality articles before playbook launch | Three articles is the target, not 5 to 8. Quality matters more than volume. Content quantity must not become a bottleneck that delays the playbook launch. |
| 2026-03-04 | The Pivot Story is the first piece of content published | "How I built an entire AI automation agency and why I stopped before signing a single client." Published first, distributed to communities first. It is authentic, establishes credibility, and naturally leads to the first playbook. |
| 2026-03-04 | Minimal site launches first | Homepage, blog, and email capture are the only pages needed at launch. Full site pages (about page, playbooks listing page, updated privacy/terms) are deferred to Phase 4 after the first playbook is live. |
| 2026-03-04 | Static site generator for the new site (Astro or 11ty) | Static HTML is too hard to maintain with a growing blog. A static site generator keeps the speed and simplicity while making regular article publishing manageable. Deploys to Netlify. Specific tool to be selected during Phase 1. |
| 2026-03-04 | Membership flip at 5 to 10 playbooks ($49/month) | Not before the library justifies the recurring price. Target: 100 members at $49/month = $4,900/month recurring. |
| 2026-03-04 | Tool/platform agnosticism is the differentiator | Playbooks and content recommend whichever tools actually work best for the problem. Not locked to one platform. The market is flooded with generic content; the void is practical, tested, step-by-step implementation. |
| 2026-03-04 | Keyword research informs playbook topics after the first one | First playbook topic is already defined (agency automation). Future topics should be validated by search demand and audience interest. |
| 2026-03-04 | Legacy files preserved in WM_Legacy/ | All agency-era files are kept for reference and as source material. None deleted. The new 00 - Context/ folder is the authoritative source going forward. |
| 2026-03-04 | Static site generator: Astro | Astro selected over 11ty. Built-in content collections, SEO integrations (sitemap, RSS), and component model are a better fit for a content-driven site that will grow with regular blog posts. Deploys to Netlify with a single config file. |
