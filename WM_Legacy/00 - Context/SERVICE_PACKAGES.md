## Pricing Philosophy

WorkMultiple prices on **value delivered, not hours worked**. The fact that we build on a no-code platform (Cowork) means our delivery time is fast, but we do not discount for speed. Clients are paying for the outcome: workflows that run without them, built by someone who understands their business, and delivered in a format their team can actually inherit and manage.

We price to be an obvious yes for our target market (high-ticket specialized contractors doing $1M-$15M with 2-25 employees). The engagement should feel like a fraction of the value it delivers.

**We do not publish pricing on the website.** Every engagement starts with a discovery call. This lets us qualify the prospect, understand their situation, and position the right scope.

### Founding Partner Program

**First 5 clients get 3 agents instead of 2, at the full $4,500 price.**

Founding partners receive a third custom agent included in The Buildout at no additional cost. This is a $2,000 bonus, not a discount. The price stays anchored at $4,500 from day one so that every testimonial and case study reinforces the real price point.

In exchange, founding partners commit to:
- A written testimonial after the engagement
- Permission to use their results as a case study (anonymized if preferred)
- Willingness to be referenced in future sales conversations

This is real scarcity. Once 5 founding partner spots are filled, the standard Buildout includes 2 agents.

**Why a bonus agent instead of a discount:** A reduced price ($2,500 or $3,500) risks cheapening the offer and making prospects suspicious of quality. Contractors respect getting more for their money. Three agents also produce a more comprehensive deployment, which means stronger case studies. "We deployed three AI agents that saved 15 hours a week" is a better story than two agents at a discount.

### The Guarantee: Scope-of-Work Guarantee

Mentioned during Call 2 (the Blueprint & Close), not published on the website:

"Everything we agree to in the workflow playbook is guaranteed. We iterate, debug, and refine until the agents flawlessly execute the exact tasks defined in the playbook. No extra cost, no scope negotiation."

This is a **completion guarantee**, not a satisfaction guarantee. The difference matters. Satisfaction is subjective and gives the client an indefinite escape hatch. Completion is objective and tied to the documented scope.

**How it works:**
- The workflow playbook created during the audit defines exactly what each agent will do, including inputs, triggers, outputs, and success criteria.
- Anything in the playbook that does not work as designed gets rebuilt at no additional cost.
- If a client requests functionality beyond the playbook scope after deployment, that is a change order (add-on agent at $2,000 or retainer work).
- The 30-day priority support window is the boundary for this guarantee. After 30 days, iteration on the original scope rolls into the retainer.

**Why this is stronger than a refund guarantee:**
- A refund guarantee signals "this might not work." A completion guarantee signals "we do not stop until it does."
- It eliminates the risk of losing 14+ days of work to a full refund.
- It prevents a client from receiving a completed product for free.
- It is a selling point, not a safety net. In Call 2, it becomes: "You are not paying for a product and hoping it works. You are paying for a defined outcome."

## Engagement Flow: Two-Call Close System

The sales process uses a strict two-call close. Jumping a prospect straight from a 15-minute introduction to a $4,500 invoice is too large a leap, especially for high-ticket contractors who are inherently skeptical of new software. The two-call structure separates the diagnosis from the prescription so the contractor feels heard before they feel sold to.

### Stage 1: Call 1 — The 15-Minute Triage (Free)

**What it is:** A 15-minute video call focused exclusively on qualification and pain discovery. Do not extend this call. Contractors respect people who respect their time, and a 15-minute commitment is a very low-friction "yes."

**This is NOT a sales call.** It is a qualification and pain-discovery call.

**The goal:** Find their "bleeding neck" and confirm they have the budget and authority to fix it.

**Pre-call intake:** The booking form collects name, email, website, and "What's taking your team the most time right now?" Consider adding: "How many active projects are you managing right now?" This gives context before the call starts. If someone says "12 active projects with a 6-person team," you already know where the pain is.

**Call flow:**
1. **Brief intro (1-2 minutes).** One sentence on what WorkMultiple does: "We build AI agents that handle the operational work burying contractor teams, things like lead follow-up, client updates, and sub coordination." Then move to questions.
2. **Targeted questions (8-10 minutes).** Ask about their lead flow and administrative bottlenecks. Are they losing track of bids? Is the office manager spending hours a day on manual data entry? How do they handle client updates during a project? What tools are they using? Listen for pain points that map to automatable workflows.
3. **The pivot (3-4 minutes).** Once they articulate the pain, stop. Do not pitch the whole package. Say: "I'm seeing exactly where the bottleneck is. We've built tools that solve this exact problem. Let me take these notes, map out a custom workflow for your specific setup, and let's get back on the calendar for 45 minutes on [specific day] to review the blueprint."

**What we learn:** What workflows are eating their time, how their team operates, what tools they use (Buildertrend, CoConstruct, etc.), whether they have budget and authority.

**What they learn:** That we understand contractor operations specifically, not AI generically. That we are going to do homework before asking for money.

**Outcome:** Book Call 2.

### The Gap: Asynchronous Value Delivery

Between Call 1 and Call 2, we deliver a custom asset that proves we understand their specific business before they have paid anything. This is the bridge that builds trust and justifies the $4,500 price.

**The asset:** A customized before/after workflow diagram built from a reusable template. The diagram visually maps their current broken process (e.g., lead emails sit for 2 days, no follow-up, lost revenue) and the WorkMultiple solution (e.g., lead emails are automatically extracted, routed to CRM, instant follow-up triggered). Customized per prospect in 15-20 minutes using notes from Call 1.

**Delivery strategy (SMS-first):** Contractors live on their phones, not their laptops. An email will get buried under supplier invoices and customer inquiries. The strategy:
1. Send the diagram via email so they can view it on a larger screen.
2. Immediately send a text message to drive their attention to it: "Hey [Name], just sent over the workflow blueprint we talked about. Take a look when you get a chance and I'll walk you through it on [day]. [booking link]"

**If the prospect goes cold:** If they do not book Call 2 within 48 hours, send one single follow-up. SMS with the diagram attached and a direct booking link. This is the only follow-up. One professional check-in maintains positioning as an in-demand consultant. Chasing a prospect makes us look like a desperate vendor.

**Cold pipeline nurture:** Prospects who do not convert after the follow-up go into a nurture list. A gentle automated check-in fires 30 or 60 days later. This is the same underlying workflow logic as the revenue leak auditor tools we sell to clients. Our own pipeline management is a live proof point for the product.

### Stage 2: Call 2 — The Blueprint & Close (45 Minutes)

**What it is:** The presentation of the tailored solution and the close. Because we have already diagnosed the problem and proven we understand their operations, we are now presenting a prescription, not a generic pitch.

**Call flow:**
1. **The walkthrough (15-20 minutes).** Present the workflow blueprint promised during Call 1. Walk through the before/after diagram. Explain how the custom agents will live on their desktop and execute this exact workflow map.
2. **The value anchor (5-10 minutes).** Frame the $4,500 against what they are currently losing. If the agents catch just one abandoned estimate that their office manager forgot to follow up on, the system has paid for itself multiple times over in week one.
3. **The pitch (10-15 minutes).** Detail the deliverables: the two custom agents (three for founding partners), deployment, team training, the playbook, 30-day support, and the scope-of-work guarantee. Present the value stack ($12,000+ in standalone value at $4,500). Mention the guarantee: "Everything we agree to in the workflow playbook is guaranteed. We iterate until it runs exactly as designed."
4. **The close (5 minutes).** Ask for the 50% upfront deposit to begin the deep-dive audit and technical build. The remaining 50% is invoiced and due immediately following successful deployment and team walkthrough.

**For founding partners:** "We are onboarding five founding partners who get a third agent included at the standard price. In exchange, we ask for a short testimonial, permission to write up a case study, and the right to use your company as a reference. After those five spots fill, the standard package is two agents at the same price."

**If they ask for a demo during Call 2:** Keep it brief (under 5 minutes). Pull up the WorkMultiple pipeline as a quick proof point. Save the detailed, tailored demo for the team walkthrough after deployment.

**Payment structure:**
- 50% upfront ($2,250) to lock in the engagement and begin the audit and technical build. This deposit is non-refundable.
- 50% ($2,250) invoiced and due immediately following successful deployment and team walkthrough.
- For founding partners: same 50/50 split at $4,500 ($2,250 / $2,250).

### Stage 3: The Buildout ($4,500)

**Timeline:** 2-3 weeks from kickoff to live agents.

**Phase 1: Audit (Days 1-5)**
- Kickoff call (30-60 minutes). The client walks us through their 2-3 most painful workflows. Screen sharing encouraged so we can see how they use their tools.
- Offline analysis (2-3 business days). We map the workflows, identify what to automate, and design the agents.

- Configure connections to the client's tools.

**Phase 3: Deploy and Train (Days 14-21)**
- Install the plugin in the client's Cowork instance.
- 60-minute team walkthrough. Demonstrate each agent, explain triggers, show how to adjust.
- Deliver the Workflow Playbook (written documentation for the team).

**Phase 4: Support (Days 21-51)**
- 30 days of priority support. Same-business-day response for issues.
- Fine-tuning based on real usage.
- Check-in at day 30 to confirm everything is running smoothly.

## Internal Pricing Logic

**Why these numbers:**

- **$4,500 Buildout (full price):** For a contractor doing $2M/year, this is 0.2% of revenue. If the agents recover one dropped lead on a $50K project, the engagement pays for itself 10x. Even at the most conservative math (10 hours/week saved at $25/hr admin cost), the ROI is $13K/year. The engagement pays for itself in under 5 months.

- **Founding partner bonus (3rd agent included):** The value of founding partners isn't just the revenue; it's the testimonials, case studies, and referrals they generate. Five founding partners with comprehensive deployments (3 agents each) create stronger case studies and social proof that justifies $4,500+ for every client after. Keeping the price at $4,500 also means every founding reference validates the real price point.

- **$2,000 add-on agents:** Lower than the per-agent value in The Buildout because the audit is already done. The marginal cost of building an additional agent for an existing client is lower.

- **$500-$1,000/month retainers:** Within comfortable range for contractors doing $1M-$15M. The Growth tier ($1,000/month) includes a quarterly agent build worth $2,000, making it a clear value proposition for clients who want to keep expanding automation.

**Our differentiation in pricing conversations:**

1. **Done-for-you, not self-serve.** Clients don't configure software. We come in, learn their workflows, and build agents that handle them. They inherit something that works, not something they need to figure out.

2. **Speed.** From kickoff to live agents in 2-3 weeks. Not months.

3. **We use what we sell.** Our own business runs on the same agents we deploy for clients.

4. **Niche expertise.** We know contractor workflows. We're not learning your industry on your dime.

## Quoting Guidelines

**Quoting happens on Call 2 (Blueprint & Close), not Call 1.** Call 1 is qualification and pain discovery only. No pricing discussed.

**On Call 2:**
- Present The Buildout as a package with the value stack: "The Buildout includes a full workflow audit, two custom agents, deployment, a team walkthrough, 30 days of support, and a written playbook. It is $4,500, and it typically pays for itself within the first month from recovered leads alone."
- For founding partners: "We are onboarding five founding partners who get a third agent included at the standard price. In exchange, we ask for a testimonial, a case study, and reference permission. [X] spots are still open."
- Present the scope-of-work guarantee: "Everything we agree to in the workflow playbook is guaranteed. We iterate until it runs exactly as designed."
- Close with the 50/50 payment structure: "50% upfront to start the audit and build, 50% after successful deployment and walkthrough."
- If they need more than 2 agents (or 3 for founding partners), mention the $2,000 add-on pricing.

**In a proposal:**
- Lead with the problem and the expected outcome, not the price.
- Include estimated time savings and recovered revenue where possible.
- Break down the value stack (show the $12,000+ in standalone value).

**Discounting rules:**
- There are no discounts. The founding partner bonus (3rd agent) is the only special offer, and it is at full price.
- Never discount add-on agents below $2,000.
- Retainer pricing is fixed. No discounts on retainers.
