## What WorkMultiple Is (The Pivot)

WorkMultiple started as a done-for-you AI agency targeting high-ticket contractors. Over the course of building the agency (brand, website, ICP, pricing, sales process, plugin architecture), I realized the agency model doesn't fit me. The reasons:

1. Each client engagement was fully custom (opposite of "build once, sell many")
2. The sales process required heavy outbound and two-call closes — I'm a marketer, not a salesperson
3. The contractor tools (Buildertrend, ServiceTitan, etc.) don't have MCP connectors in Claude Cowork, creating integration gaps I can't control
4. The stress of managing client expectations and delivering bespoke builds was already weighing on me before even reaching out to a single client

**WorkMultiple is now pivoting to a content-driven authority brand that helps non-technical people use AI to get more done.** The tagline "More done. Same team." still applies. The domain (workmultiple.com) and visual identity are being kept. The business model is changing completely.

## The New Business Model: The Ecosystem Approach

### Phase 1: The Playbook Engine (Short-Term Revenue)

Instead of building automation systems for one client at a time, I document the builds as premium step-by-step playbooks ($197–$497 each). Each playbook solves one specific business problem using AI tools. They are implementation-ready: exact steps, exact tool settings, exact prompts, screenshots of every screen, troubleshooting guides. The buyer is paying for 20+ hours of trial-and-error they don't have to do themselves.

**Target audience:** Non-technical founders, entrepreneurs, small business operators, and employees who want to use AI beyond ChatGPT but don't know how. People who want to speed up processes or growth, don't want to be left behind, but don't know how to use AI to accomplish this.

**Playbook pricing:** $197–$497 per playbook depending on depth and complexity.

**Revenue math target:** 10 customers at $2K each, or 40 customers at $500 each = $20K. This is achievable once a library of 8–10 playbooks exists with steady organic traffic.

### Phase 2: The Authority Hub (Long-Term Growth)

As I build playbooks, I naturally test different platforms, compare automation frameworks, and troubleshoot integrations. I publish those findings as free content on the WorkMultiple site — deep-dive comparisons, tool reviews, practical tutorials. This is NOT a generic "Top 10 AI Tools" directory. It's opinionated, tested, practical content like "I tried to automate client follow-up with 4 different AI tools. Here's what actually worked."

Free content drives SEO traffic. Each article includes affiliate links for tools reviewed. Each article also serves as top-of-funnel that points readers to relevant premium playbooks.

### Phase 3: Membership Flip (Scale)

Once there are 5–10 solid playbooks, flip the model into a $49/month premium community. Gate the playbooks and ongoing tool reviews behind a recurring subscription. Target: 100 members at $49/month = $4,900/month recurring, plus affiliate commissions that compound as traffic grows.

## Actionable Refinements to Incorporate

The following refinements were agreed upon during strategy discussions and should be treated as requirements in the new context files:

1. **Early traffic strategy matters.** SEO won't rank for months. Post the free comparison content in these communities, build credibility, and link to the playbook. This is marketing, not sales — no DMs, no outbound.

2. **Playbook quality bar is high.** At $197–$497, the playbook must be genuinely implementation-ready. Not "here's what you could do" but "here are the exact steps, the exact tool settings, the exact prompts, screenshots of every screen, and a troubleshooting guide for common failures." The buyer is paying for 20+ hours of trial-and-error they don't have to do.

3. **Don't lock to one platform.** Playbooks should solve specific business problems using whichever tools actually work best for that problem. WorkMultiple becomes the brand that tests everything and tells you what actually works. This also maximizes affiliate revenue across multiple tool ecosystems.

4. **Tool/platform agnosticism is the differentiator.** The market is flooded with generic "Top 10 ChatGPT Prompts" guides. The void is practical, technical, step-by-step implementation tutorials that actually test and compare tools against real business outcomes.

5. **The first playbook ("How to Build a Done-for-You AI Automation Agency") should be priced at $297–$497** given the depth of material available from the agency build. It covers niche selection, ICP development, pricing strategy, sales process design, technical architecture, client delivery, and the integration landscape.

6. **Keyword research should inform playbook topics after the first one.** Identify what non-technical founders and entrepreneurs are actively searching for related to AI automation, workflow optimization, and AI tool implementation.
