If you're building a newsletter and want to make money from it without juggling multiple tools, Beehiiv is purpose-built for exactly that. The platform was created by the founders of Morning Brew after scaling that newsletter to 4 million subscribers. It combines email, web hosting, analytics, and access to a native ad network in one dashboard. But is it the right choice for your newsletter business? Let's break down what Beehiiv actually does, what it costs, and who should use it.

## What Beehiiv Does

Beehiiv is a newsletter platform that handles the entire creator economy stack. You write and send emails to your subscribers, host your newsletter on a web page that Beehiiv hosts for you, analyze how your subscribers engage with your content, and monetize through multiple channels (paid subscriptions, sponsorships, and ads). The platform also includes audience growth tools to help you add subscribers without buying ads.

The key insight is this: Beehiiv doesn't make you piece together separate tools for newsletters, websites, analytics, and ad networks. Everything runs from one account, and your data flows between features instead of siloing.

## Who It's For

Beehiiv works best for newsletter creators and small publishing operations that want to monetize. Specifically:

- **Solo newsletter writers** building a business around their writing, not just sharing updates.
- **Content creators** (coaches, consultants, journalists) who use email as the core distribution channel.
- **Small publishing teams** (2-5 people) managing one or more newsletters.
- **Bloggers and journalists** transitioning from personal blogs or platforms like Medium.

It's less suited for:

- **B2B marketing teams** that need advanced segmentation, multi-step automation, and deep CRM integration.
- **E-commerce businesses** relying on email for customer retention and abandoned cart sequences (Beehiiv lacks strong integrations with Shopify and similar platforms).
- **Bootstrapped creators on a tight budget** who can't absorb the $43+ monthly cost yet.

## Key Features

### Newsletter Editor and Templates

Beehiiv includes a drag-and-drop editor with pre-built templates. The design flexibility is clean but limited: Beehiiv offers about 7 core layouts, which is fewer than competitors like MailerLite. Templates are modern and professional, but if you need highly customized designs or want dozens of layout options, you'll feel the constraint.

### Email Segmentation

You can segment your list by subscriber engagement (opens, clicks, replies), paid vs. free status, subscriber source (which form they signed up through), custom fields, and survey responses. Segments update dynamically based on behavior or remain static for one-time campaigns. This covers the basics well but lacks the tag-based system that competitors like Kit (formerly ConvertKit) provide. You can't, for example, tag subscribers as "from coaching clients" separate from "newsletter subscribers," which limits how precisely you can target different audiences.

### Email Automations

Automations allow you to send time-triggered email sequences based on subscriber behavior or custom events. However, automations are only available on the Scale plan and up, not on the free Launch tier. This is a meaningful limitation if you're just starting: you can't automate welcome sequences or abandoned webinar reminders until you pay.

### Website and Landing Pages

Beehiiv hosts your newsletter's landing page and homepage, eliminating the need for a separate site builder for basic publishing. You get a custom domain, SEO-friendly page structure, and the ability to build landing pages for lead captures or promotions. However, the customization is limited: you can't add canonical URLs (a problem if you've written content elsewhere and want to port it), and Beehiiv doesn't leverage domain authority from established sites. For pure writing platforms, it works. For SEO-heavy publishers, it falls short.

### Analytics

Beehiiv shows open rates, click-through rates, subscriber growth, churn, and engagement trends over time. The "3D analytics" dashboard visualizes how subscriber engagement correlates with growth. But detailed tracking has limits: the platform only reports traffic source by medium (organic, email, direct), not by specific referrer or campaign source. For simple needs, it's clear. For detailed attribution, it's underwhelming.

### Growth Tools

**Recommendations:** A cross-promotion feature where newsletters recommend each other to grow their lists organically (opt-in, no cost to users).

**Boosts:** A paid sponsorship marketplace where you can pay to have other creators recommend your newsletter to their audiences. Effective for buying list growth but not free.

**Referral Program:** Built-in referral system for creators to incentivize word-of-mouth growth.

These tools address a real problem for newsletter creators: organic growth is slow. But Boost essentially lets you buy subscribers through influencer promotions, which works but costs money.

### Monetization Features

- **Paid subscriptions:** Charge readers for premium content. Beehiiv takes no cut beyond standard payment processing fees.
- **Sponsorships and ads:** Sell ad placements in your emails and website.
- **Newsletter ads:** Access to Beehiiv's native ad network. Beehiiv matches brands with newsletters, and you earn per ad slot.

This monetization stack is Beehiiv's biggest differentiator. Substack charges a 10% cut on subscription revenue. Beehiiv doesn't. If you're serious about turning your newsletter into a revenue source, this matters.

## Pricing Breakdown

Beehiiv uses subscriber count tiers, not email volume tiers. You pay for how many people are on your list, not how many emails you send.

### Launch (Free)
- Up to 2,500 subscribers
- Unlimited email sends
- Website and landing pages
- Basic analytics
- Beehiiv branding on your site
- No automations
- No paid subscriptions or monetization features

The free tier is genuinely useful if you're under 2,500 subscribers and don't need automations or monetization. It's not a crippled trial; it's a real product.

### Scale Plan
- Starts at $43/month (for up to 1,000 subscribers)
- Scales with subscriber count (pricing increases as you add subscribers)
- Includes: email automations, paid subscriptions, sponsored content, access to ad network, A/B testing, AI features
- Removes Beehiiv branding

Pricing details beyond 1,000 subscribers: Beehiiv doesn't publish exact tiers on their main pricing page, but the pattern is incremental: 2,500 subscribers might be $55/month, 5,000 might be $75/month, and so on. You'll see the exact price before upgrading.

### Max Plan
- Starts at $96/month (for up to 1,000 subscribers)
- Includes everything in Scale plus: priority support, advanced analytics, premium AI features, audience growth strategies

### Enterprise
- Custom pricing for 100k+ subscribers
- Includes: dedicated IP, white-label options, account manager

### Free Trial

All plans come with a 14-day free trial of Scale or Max (your choice) with no credit card required. This is a genuine advantage: you can test automations, monetization, and advanced features before committing.

**Real cost example:** If you reach 10,000 subscribers on the Scale plan, expect to pay approximately $100-120/month. If you're generating $500+ in sponsored content or subscription revenue, the platform pays for itself. If you have a small newsletter, it might not.

## Strengths

**1. Unified platform for creators.** Most newsletter platforms handle email. Beehiiv handles email, hosting, analytics, and monetization together. You don't fragment your data across tools.

**2. No cut on subscription revenue.** Unlike Substack's 10% fee, Beehiiv takes nothing on paid subscriptions (only standard payment processing fees). For a creator earning $5,000/month in subscriptions, that's $500 in your pocket every month instead of Substack's.

**3. Growth tools included.** Recommendations, Boosts, and the referral program are built in. You're not buying a separate growth service on top of the email platform.

**4. Generous free tier.** The Launch plan is actually usable up to 2,500 subscribers. Many competitors lock core features (automations, segmentation) behind a paywall from day one.

**5. AI features integrated.** Use AI to draft emails, generate subject lines, or build landing pages. It's not the deepest AI integration on the market, but it's useful and requires no additional tool.

**6. Strong community and momentum.** Beehiiv has cultivated a creator community and offers resources (courses, case studies, webinars) to help you grow. The platform is actively developed and listening to its user base.

## Weaknesses

**1. Limited email design control.** Seven templates is restrictive if you want visual variety across campaigns. If professional design is critical, you'll feel the constraint.

**2. Weak segmentation compared to competitors.** No tag system means you can't granularly organize subscribers into overlapping groups. This is fine for simple newsletters but limits B2B or multi-audience use cases.

**3. Automation is limited and starts at Scale tier.** Beehiiv's automation triggers don't match Kit's flexibility. If you need complex, multi-step sequences, you may outgrow the platform quickly. And if you're on the free tier, you get no automations at all.

**4. Shared domain deliverability issues.** All Beehiiv users send from shared domains. In 2025-2026, reports emerged of spam complaints from other Beehiiv users affecting the entire platform's deliverability. This is Beehiiv's responsibility to fix, but it's a real issue if your emails land in spam through no fault of your own.

**5. No direct video upload.** You can't upload videos directly to Beehiiv; you have to embed them from external sources using HTML. This is clunky and time-consuming.

**6. Poor integration with e-commerce.** If you're selling products through Shopify, Beehiiv doesn't integrate natively like Klaviyo or Omnisend do. You'll need workarounds.

**7. Free plan is stripped of monetization.** The Launch tier excludes automations, paid subscriptions, and ads. This pushes users toward the Scale plan faster than they might expect.

**8. Email size can trigger clipping.** Beehiiv's HTML rendering sometimes bloats email code, hitting Gmail's 102kb clipping limit and cutting off your beautifully crafted newsletter mid-sentence.

## Alternatives

If Beehiiv isn't the right fit, consider:

- **Substack:** Best if you want simplicity and are willing to accept a 10% cut on subscription revenue. Completely free. Great for solo writers.
- **Kit (formerly ConvertKit):** Best for creators who need advanced automation, sales funnels, and digital product integrations. Pricier than Beehiiv but more powerful for marketing-heavy operations.
- **MailerLite:** Best for a balance of features and affordability. Strong segmentation, automation, and a generous free plan. Less focused on monetization but more flexible overall.
- **Campaign Monitor:** Best for teams that want professional email marketing with advanced automation and deliverability. Steeper learning curve, but powerful.

## Verdict

**Use Beehiiv if:**
- You're a creator, writer, or journalist building a newsletter business.
- Monetization (paid subscriptions, sponsorships, ads) is a core goal.
- You want one platform instead of juggling email, landing pages, and analytics tools.
- You're willing to pay $43+ per month and can reach breakeven on subscription or sponsorship revenue.
- Simple to moderate email design and segmentation are sufficient.

**Don't use Beehiiv if:**
- You're a B2B marketer needing complex segmentation, advanced CRM integration, and sophisticated automation.
- You're running a tight startup with no budget yet (use Substack free tier instead).
- You need maximum email design control and dozens of template options.
- You're selling physical products and need deep e-commerce integration.
- You operate on a shared domain and are sensitive to deliverability issues outside your control.

Beehiiv is the best platform for creators who want to turn their newsletter into a revenue stream without managing a complex tech stack. The monetization features and unified dashboard genuinely differentiate it from the alternatives. Just be realistic: the platform expects you to grow to at least a few hundred paying subscribers to justify the cost. If you're experimenting or just sharing updates, Substack free or MailerLite free will serve you better.
